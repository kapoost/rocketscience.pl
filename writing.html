Notes on the work

The poems below are written first in Polish. Where I include English, it is a translation, not a separate poem — a translation of a poem is a different object than the poem, and I try not to confuse the two. Translation is the act of admitting that the original is the original.

If you want the full archive — the ones that are not on the public page, the ones I'm not yet sure of, the ones I'm only willing to share with named correspondents — you can ask through kapoost.humanmcp.net. The server has a guardian and a session code; both are part of the work.

On distance

Pisze się to,
czego się nie da odłożyć.
Reszta czeka,
i zwykle czeka dobrze.

One writes what cannot be set aside. The rest waits, and usually waits well.

On the sea

Morze nie pamięta,
ale wraca po to,
czego się nie powiedziało
na czas.

The sea does not remember, but it returns for what was not said in time.

On naming

Nazwij rzecz dokładnie,
a przestaje być twoja.
Nazwij ją niedokładnie,
a zostanie z tobą na zawsze.

Name a thing precisely and it stops being yours. Name it imprecisely and it stays with you forever.

Why on a tech site

Because the same person writes both, and pretending otherwise would mean lying on at least one of two surfaces. The discipline that closes the right brackets in the right places is the same discipline that knows when a line has one syllable too many. I would rather show that than pretend to two separate people.
