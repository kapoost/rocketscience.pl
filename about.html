About

A sailor. A newbie poet. A beginning musician. A CTO. Everything on this site comes from real experience — the kind that takes longer than you planned.

The short version

I'm Łukasz Kapuśniak, online as kapoost. I'm the CTO of Cloud Technologies, a company built around audience data, signal processing, and the unglamorous plumbing that makes targeted advertising legible to the rest of the stack.

Outside of that day job — though the lines are not as clean as I'd like — I live between three things: the sea, the code, and the verse. Each of them keeps the others honest.

What I work on

My professional focus for the past few years has been the Ad Context Protocol: an open spec that lets AI agents discover, plan, and execute advertising directly with publishers. I build seller-side agents (one of them is Purrsonality), contribute storyboards and schema fixes upstream, and run a small signal-provider stack for the kind of data Cloud Technologies has been refining for years.

Underneath that, the work I find most satisfying is the boring kind: getting error codes right, choosing the right name for a status field, deciding what should be a 4xx versus a 5xx, what should be silently retried versus surfaced to a human. The decisions that, when correct, are invisible.

The sea

I sail. Not professionally — I'm still on the upward side of the learning curve where every passage teaches a small humbling lesson about the difference between charted depths and actual depths. The sea is where I learn that intuition is mostly accumulated mistakes that haven't killed you yet, and that the wind doesn't care about your plan.

Some of the writing on this site is from the boat. Most of it is from later, when the boat is moored and the body remembers what the mind was too busy to record.

The verse

I write poems, mostly in Polish, mostly short. I'm a beginner — but I take it seriously enough to refuse the easy lines and to throw away most of what I write. A small page of writing on this site collects the ones that survived.

The poems and the code are not the same activity, but they have a similar discipline: choose the right word, kill the rest, leave space around what remains.

The music

Beginner here too. Mostly piano. The instrument is patient with me; the metronome less so.

Why this site exists

I needed a stable address for the projects I want to point at — Purrsonality, the AdCP work, humanMCP — and I wanted that address to belong to me, not to a platform. Rocketscience.pl is mine, written in plain HTML, served from a small Cloudflare edge, and it will outlive whatever framework is fashionable when you read this.

It is also the canonical home for purrsonality.rocketscience.pl, where the cat-personality quiz now lives.
